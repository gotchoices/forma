## 1. The question

The standard-model-plus-graviton picture treats the
graviton as a **fundamental** spin-2 gauge boson on a fixed
Minkowski background, quantized the same way the photon is
quantized from A_μ.  It is perturbatively well-defined
below roughly M_Pl and non-renormalizable above it; all
standard UV completions (string theory, loop quantum
gravity, asymptotic safety, causal dynamical triangulations)
are attempts to patch that divergence by adding extra
structure above the Planck scale.

MaSt unifies electromagnetism and gravity at the
metric-off-diagonal level in a different way: A_μ is the
KK reduction of an Ma-tube off-diagonal, and h_μν is the
transverse-traceless perturbation of the large-scale
metric that GRID's lattice statistics produces.  The two
are parallel as 4D fields but do not have parallel
origins.  Specifically, the photon has a **promotion-layer
home** (the aleph-S¹ closure of [Q131](Q131-dark-energy-as-unpromoted-information.md)),
while the graviton has no corresponding compact-cycle
closure — it arises from the collective thermodynamics of
the lattice itself.

The question:  **what kind of object is a MaSt graviton,
and how does that reading differ from the standard
SM-plus-graviton picture?**

## 2. The proposal

**The graviton is a collective excitation of the GRID
lattice — structurally closer to a phonon in a solid than
to a photon on a fixed background.**

> h_μν is not a field living *on* a spacetime; it is a
> long-wavelength perturbation *of* the emergent
> large-scale metric that GRID's lattice dynamics produce
> (`grid/gravity.md`).  Its quantization gives genuine
> quanta with energy ℏω — gravitons exist as
> long-wavelength excitations — but these quanta are
> collective modes of the substrate, not promoted
> compact-cycle eigenstates.  The graviton description is
> valid below the lattice scale (roughly M_Pl) and
> dissolves into GRID dynamics above it.  There is no
> trans-Planckian graviton, because there is no
> trans-Planckian long-wavelength.  The UV completion of
> gravity is GRID itself, not a stringy or loopy patch.

This is the direct reading of the existing GRID gravity
derivation (ζ = 1/4 and tetrahedral packing produce G;
linearized Einstein equations emerge from lattice
thermodynamics).  It is formulated here as a statement
about the graviton's *ontological rank* because that is
the statement SM-native readers need.

the promoter.

## 4. What the reading derives

Four things that are parameters or unsolved problems in
SM-plus-graviton become derivations or structural features
under this reading:

### 4.1 — Newton's constant G

GRID's tetrahedral packing combined with the information
density ζ = 1/4 (matching Bekenstein–Hawking entropy
coefficient per unit Planck area) fixes G from first
principles.  The derivation lives in `grid/gravity.md`
and `grid/synthesis.md`; its structural status is that G
is not an input to the theory but a consequence of the
lattice geometry.  This is MaSt/GRID framework content,
not standard physics.

### 4.2 — UV finiteness of gravity

Because the graviton is a collective lattice mode, its
description dissolves into direct GRID dynamics at
wavelengths approaching the lattice spacing.  There are
no trans-Planckian gravitons to contribute divergent
loops, so the non-renormalizability pathology of
SM-plus-graviton does not arise.  The built-in cutoff is
not imposed; it is the regime boundary of the phonon-like
description.

This is directly parallel to how phonons in a solid stop
being meaningful at wavelengths approaching the atomic
lattice spacing.  It is an appeal to a structural feature
of the MaSt/GRID framework (the lattice), not to any
new physics added on top.

### 4.3 — Dark energy magnitude (in principle)

The cosmological-constant puzzle under MaSt/GRID is
carried by [Q131](Q131-dark-energy-as-unpromoted-information.md).
It remains unsolved in magnitude, but is
structurally located:  Λ is sourced by unpromoted
aleph-phase residue, not by graviton loops.  The
SM-plus-graviton picture has no analogous structural
location for Λ — graviton zero-point sums give the
standard catastrophic ~10¹²³ overestimate, with no
mechanism to cut it.

### 4.4 — Black-hole entropy from microstate count

In MaSt/GRID, S_BH = A/4 is literally a count of GRID
tetrahedra at the horizon.  This is a concrete microstate
picture with a small ontological commitment (just the
lattice), reproducing Bekenstein–Hawking at the same
coefficient.  In SM-plus-graviton (without string theory
or LQG), S_BH is thermodynamic input with no microstate
model.

## 5. What the reading predicts and forbids

### 5.1 — Graviton has no compact-dimension charge

Unlike the photon (which couples to Ma-tube winding as
charge; Q132) or Ma-bound matter (whose ring-winding is
mass), the graviton carries no compact-cycle quantum
number.  It couples to the total stress-energy tensor
T_μν universally with strength 1/M_Pl².

**Forbidden:**  "graviton families" parameterized by Ma
sheet (there is no per-sheet graviton), "generational
graviton" coupling variations, "graviton charge" of any
kind.  The graviton is 4D-only.

### 5.2 — Graviton description has a hard high-energy cutoff

Near the lattice scale, the phonon-like description
dissolves.  Phenomenologically:

- Graviton–graviton scattering at tree level matches
  standard EFT calculations below M_Pl; loop corrections
  do not blow up, because the loop integrals are cut off
  by the lattice discreteness at scales a user of the
  EFT would have had to regulate by hand.
- Dispersion relations for h_μν may pick up
  lattice-discreteness corrections at very short
  wavelengths.  In principle observable via ultra-high-
  frequency gravitational wave searches or very long
  baseline timing experiments; in practice well beyond
  current sensitivity.
- Lorentz violation signatures near the Planck scale are
  possible but are strongly constrained by existing
  observations (Fermi-LAT timing of γ-ray bursts, UHECR
  thresholds); MaSt/GRID must satisfy those bounds and,
  in its currently formulated emergent-Lorentz-invariance
  structure, is expected to.

### 5.3 — No "dark graviton" sector

The parallel Q131/Q125 argument does **not** apply to the
graviton.  Dark energy is level-0 content that failed
aleph promotion; dark matter is sheet-layer content that
formed a T⁶ mode but failed spin-alignment.  Neither is
"a graviton that failed something."  The graviton sits on
a different layer and cannot have a dark counterpart of
the same kind.

This is a specific MaSt/GRID forbidding.  SM-plus-graviton
has no such rule — it could in principle entertain
mirror-gravity sectors or hidden-sector gravitons without
contradiction.

### 5.4 — Quantum gravity at trans-Planck scale is not a graviton problem

The research program "quantize the graviton field above
M_Pl" is replaced by "understand GRID's lattice dynamics."
These are different problems:  the former seeks a UV
completion of a spin-2 gauge theory; the latter asks for
the microscopic statistics of a discrete causal lattice
with ζ = 1/4.  The second is the program MaSt/GRID is
already executing (see `grid/` directory); the first does
not exist as a MaSt research target.

## 6. Placement vs. existing quantum-gravity programs

On the spectrum between "graviton is fundamental" and
"graviton is fully emergent," MaSt/GRID sits at the
emergent end, in company with specific prior work but not
identical to any of it:

- **Fundamental-graviton programs** (perturbative quantum
  gravity, stringy UV completions treating the graviton
  as a closed-string mode at low energies):  MaSt/GRID
  disagrees that the graviton is a fundamental field on a
  background.  It agrees that h_μν has spin-2 quanta at
  low energies.
- **Loop quantum gravity** (quantizes spin-network states
  of geometry directly):  structurally sympathetic — LQG
  also treats geometry as the primary quantum object.
  MaSt/GRID differs in that its substrate is a causal
  lattice with fixed ζ and a specific thermodynamic
  derivation of G, rather than SU(2) spin networks.
- **Asymptotic safety** (fundamental graviton reaching a
  non-trivial UV fixed point):  MaSt/GRID does not need
  such a fixed point — the cutoff is physical, not a
  renormalization-group construction.
- **Emergent / entropic gravity** (Jacobson 1995;
  Verlinde 2010, 2016; Padmanabhan):  closest in spirit.
  MaSt/GRID is a concrete instance:  it specifies *what*
  substrate gives Einstein's equations (GRID lattice
  with ζ = 1/4), *why* it gives the Bekenstein–Hawking
  coefficient (tetrahedral packing saturating the entropy
  bound), and *how* the emergent graviton relates to the
  other emergent bosons (same metric, different
  off-diagonals, different promotion layers).
- **Causal set theory** (Sorkin):  sympathetic; MaSt/GRID
  shares the discrete causal structure but adds the
  compact-dimension ℵ line and Ma sheets for matter
  content.
- **Superfluid / analog-gravity models** (Volovik, Zloshchastiev):
  phonons-in-superfluid is the same mathematical pattern.
  MaSt/GRID's substrate is explicitly a causal information
  lattice rather than a physical superfluid, but the
  graviton-as-phonon reading is the same pattern.

The novel content MaSt/GRID offers over the entropic-
gravity and analog-gravity programs is the **unification
with the rest of physics**:  not just gravity as emergent,
but gauge fields as emergent KK reductions of the same
lattice metric, and matter as promoted modes on compact
cycles of the same structure.  The graviton is not
singled out; it is one of several emergent objects, and it
happens to sit one layer below the promoted ones.

## 7. What the reading owes as derivations

This file captures a position; it does not finish it.
Concrete derivation targets:

1. **Dispersion relation for h_μν from GRID statistics.**
   Linearized Einstein equations are derived in
   `grid/gravity.md`; the graviton mode's dispersion
   (ω = c|k| at low k, plus any lattice corrections at
   large k) should come out of the same thermodynamic
   chain.  Quantifying the cutoff scale and its functional
   form is the main technical task.
2. **Explicit phonon quantization.**  Given the
   dispersion, standard second-quantization gives a
   graviton with ℏω energy per mode.  Writing this down
   formally makes the "phonon" analogy a theorem rather
   than a slogan, and lets the forbidden predictions of
   §5 be stated with exact bounds.
3. **Distinction between the aleph-line photon and the
   substrate-mode graviton.**  Both arise from "lattice
   dynamics" in some sense; the promotion-chain structure
   of Q132 says *why* the photon is first-class and the
   graviton is collective.  This argument is intuitive in
   the companion discussion but needs to be formalized
   against a specific definition of "promoted" vs.
   "substrate-emergent."
4. **Compatibility with Q131 and Q125.**  Q131's
   unpromoted phase and Q125's spin-gated dark matter
   both sit at levels *above or at* the level at which
   the graviton emerges.  A joint derivation study
   should confirm that the three pieces —
   substrate-emergent graviton, level-0 residue as dark
   energy, sheet-layer residue as dark matter — are
   mutually consistent descriptions of a single lattice.
5. **Explicit lattice-scale phenomenology.**  What, if
   anything, would distinguish MaSt/GRID's graviton from
   a fundamental one in a realistically achievable
   experiment?  Dispersion cutoffs and graviton-graviton
   scattering above ultra-high energies are standard
   candidates; black-hole ringdown spectra near
   high-curvature regimes might also carry signatures
   if the lattice spacing is at the Planck scale or
   slightly above.

  neither of which SM-plus-graviton provides.
- Forbids **dark gravitons**, **graviton families by Ma
  sheet**, and **graviton compact-dimension charge** —
  specific empirical predictions that distinguish the
  reading from the standard picture.
- Frames the **research program for quantum gravity**:
  deriving GRID's lattice dynamics, not quantizing a
  background-dependent spin-2 field.

If wrong, the reading is still useful:  it states the
MaSt/GRID position explicitly enough that reviewers and
future studies can point at specific places it could
break (§7 1–5), and progress either way.
