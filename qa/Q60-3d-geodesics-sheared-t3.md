# Q60. 3D geodesics on sheared T³: what is the charge formula?

**Status:** Answered — R19 Track 6 (F31)
**Source:** R19 Track 4/5 discussion
**Connects to:** R19, R14, Q13 (three material dimensions)
**Model era:** Model A/B

R19 Tracks 1–5 used the 2D charge formula for (1,m) modes confined to a plane of T³. But on T³, a photon can wind in all three material dimensions: (n₁, n₂, n₃) with all nᵢ ≠ 0.

Answered by R19 Track 6 (F31) — the 3D charge integral gives a selection rule: s₁₃ = 0 kills charge for n₃ ≠ 0 modes. No charged particle lighter than the electron exists on T³. Fractional charges from 3D geodesics are ruled out. See also Q63 (electron planarity).
