# Q33: Can gravity and charge both arise from photon interactions?

**Status:** Answered  
**Source:** User question  
**Related:** [Q32](Q32-energy-geometry-fundamentals.md)

**Short answer:** Gravity (energy coupling) and EM charge (winding-topology coupling)
both trace back to photon properties but to *different* properties. EM residuals
(multipoles) cannot replicate gravity's 1/r² law.

Two sub-questions:

**(a) Is there an orientation where two photons always attract?
(gravity-like)**

YES.  Two photons gravitationally attract whenever they are not
exactly co-propagating.  This is a standard GR result:
- Anti-parallel photons: attract with effective mass 2E/c²
  (Tolman-Ehrenfest-Podolsky, 1930s; factor of 2 from
  relativistic beaming of the gravitational field).
- Co-propagating photons: zero gravitational interaction
  (exact pp-wave result — the metric of a lightlike source
  is Ricci-flat along the beam direction).
- Any other angle: intermediate attraction, never repulsion.

The interaction is always attractive or zero because gravity
couples to the stress-energy tensor, and photon energy is
always positive.  This IS gravity.

**(b) Can photon EM fields selectively attract or repel?
(charge-like)**

For FREE photons: no.  Classical Maxwell equations are linear —
photons pass through each other.  (QED gives α²-suppressed
photon-photon scattering, negligible.)

For CONFINED photons (winding modes on T²/T³): YES.
A confined photon with winding +q produces a time-averaged
field that looks like positive charge.  Winding −q looks like
negative charge.  Two confined photons:
- Same winding sign → repulsion (like charges)
- Opposite winding sign → attraction (unlike charges)

The sign is determined purely by topology (winding direction).

**Summary:**

| Force | Couples to          | Source          | Sign           |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Gravity | Energy (T_μν)     | Always positive | Universal +    |
| EM      | Winding (topology)| ±q              | Sign-carrying  |

Gravity = force between energies.
EM = force between topologies.

**(c) Can EM residuals (dipole/multipole) replace gravity?**

NO.  An asymmetric toroidal charge distribution does produce
multipole fields beyond the monopole:
- Electric dipole: zero (torus has inversion symmetry)
- Magnetic dipole: nonzero (circulating photon = current
  loop — this IS the electron's magnetic moment)
- Electric quadrupole: nonzero (oblate charge distribution)

These create always-attractive residual interactions between
neutral objects (van der Waals), analogous to water molecules
glomming together.  But they CANNOT be gravity because:
1. Wrong distance law: dipole ~ 1/r³, van der Waals ~ 1/r⁶–⁷,
   but gravity is 1/r².  No multipole residual can reproduce
   the monopole's 1/r² dependence.
2. Not proportional to mass: multipole interactions depend on
   geometry (dipole moment, polarizability), not total energy.
3. Gravity acts on free photons: light bends around the sun,
   but free photons have zero charge and zero multipole moments.
   Multipole EM residuals can't explain this.

In nature, every known residual force is shorter-range than its
parent: nuclear force (residual color) decays exponentially;
van der Waals (residual EM) goes as ~1/r⁶.  Gravity at 1/r²
would require a parent force of even longer range, and no such
force is known.

**Conclusion:** Gravity is a separate coupling (energy → spacetime
curvature via GR), not a residual of EM.  Both forces trace back
to photon properties, but to *different* properties: energy
(gravity) vs. winding topology (EM).  This is arguably the
sharpest expression of Q32's principle at the force level.
