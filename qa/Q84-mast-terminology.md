# Q84: MaSt — terminology and naming conventions

**Status:** Adopted
**Full reference:** [`../studies/Taxonomy.md`](../studies/Taxonomy.md)

---

## Summary

**MaSt** = **Ma**terial – **S**pace – **t**ime.

| Symbol | Name | Dim | What it is |
|--------|------|:---:|------------|
| Ma | Material space | 6 | Compact dimensions; 3 periodic sheets where particles are standing waves |
| Ma_e | Electron sheet | 2 | Modes: electron, muon, tau |
| Ma_ν | Neutrino sheet | 2 | Modes: neutrino mass eigenstates |
| Ma_p | Proton sheet | 2 | Modes: proton, hadrons, nuclei |
| S | Space | 3 | The three large spatial dimensions |
| t | Time | 1 | Time |

Full arena: **Ma × S × t** (10 dimensions).

---

## Naming rationale

Einstein fused space and time into spacetime.  MaSt adds a
lower layer: six compact dimensions where matter IS —
particles are standing waves on these dimensions.

- **"Material"** chosen over "matter" because it functions
  as an adjective ("material dimensions," "material sheet")
- **No superscript dimension counts** — avoids collisions
  with exponents and standard math notation (S³, T¹, M²)
- **Lowercase t** — signals time is the familiar part;
  Material is the new idea

## Migration from old notation

| Old | MaSt | Improvement |
|-----|------|-------------|
| T⁶ | Ma | No collision with time; no superscript |
| T² | "material sheet" or Ma_e etc. | No collision with mass-squared |
| T²_ν | Ma_ν | Reads as "neutrino material sheet" |
| R³ | S | No superscript, no 3-sphere collision |
| R³ × T⁶ | Ma × S × t | Parses as "material, space, time" |
| "compact dimensions" | "material dimensions" | States the thesis |
| t6.py | ma.py | Consistent with framework name |
| alpha_kk | alpha_ma | Consistent with Ma notation |
