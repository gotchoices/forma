## 1. The question

The MaSt/GRID promotion ladder (Q132) carries content through
three levels:

- **Level 0** — raw lattice phase information.
- **Level 1** — aleph-S¹ resonance promotes information into a
  photon (light / energy).
- **Level 2** — Ma-T² resonance promotes a photon into bound
  matter (mass / charge).

Q131 asked what happens to **level-0 content that fails to
promote to level 1** — answer: dark energy, a structureless
vacuum-like residue.

This question asks the parallel one one rung up:

> **What happens to level-1 content (photons) that does not
> get further promoted to level 2 (bound matter)?  Is
> "free-propagating information" the right name for it, and
> is decoherence / measurement the level-1 → level-2 promotion
> event itself?**

## 2. The proposal

> A propagating photon carries level-1 content.  When the
> photon's circulation does **not** satisfy a Ma-T² closure
> condition, it remains free — a delocalized,
> information-bearing excitation rather than a bound particle.
> When the photon's circulation **does** lock onto a Ma cycle
> — typically through interaction with an existing bound mode
> or scattering target — a topological promotion event
> (an A6 vortex / KK closure) binds the content into a
> level-2 standing wave.  This binding step is what we call
> **measurement** or **decoherence**.  The wavefunction prior
> to measurement is the level-1 content itself.

This identifies, in GRID/MaSt language:

- **Wavefunction ψ** ≡ free information riding on photon-level
  content (level-1, not yet level-2).
- **Decoherence / measurement** ≡ promotion event from level 1
  to level 2 (mechanism: A6 vortex / Ma-T² closure).
- **"Collapse"** ≡ the topological binding step itself —
  a real physical event, not a postulate.

Combining with Q131 fills out the ladder's residue bookkeeping:

The level-1 residue inherits one feature its level-0 sibling

so free information at level 1 has tensor structure even though
it has no rest mass.  This is consistent with the wavefunction
being an amplitude with phase, not a scalar density.

## 4. Standard-physics anchors

This is not a new physical claim — it is a re-reading of
well-established ideas in GRID/MaSt structure.

**Quantum information theory.**  Information rides on photons;
this is the operating principle of quantum communication.
Holevo's theorem bounds the classical information extractable
from a single photon at `log₂(modes)`.  The wavefunction in this
reading is the modal information distribution carried by the
photon, not a separate epistemic object.

**Decoherence theory.**  Standard QM (Zurek, Joos, Schlosshauer):
a quantum state remains coherent until it entangles with
environmental degrees of freedom; then "decoherence" makes
definite outcomes irreversible.  In GRID language, entanglement
with the environment is precisely a Ma-T² closure event — the
photon binds into a standing wave (level-2 promotion), and the
previously-free information becomes localized.

**Landauer's principle.**  Erasing a bit costs at least
kT ln 2 of heat dissipation.  Reread in GRID: erasing a bit
means de-promoting bound (level-2) information back to free
(level-1) content.  The thermodynamic cost is the binding
energy released as the topological closure is undone.

**Black-hole information.**  The lattice-shear horizon picture
([`grid/hexagonal.md`](../grid/hexagonal.md), Schwarzschild
shearing section) topologically severs the interior from the
exterior at the event horizon.  In this reading, free (level-1)
information that crosses the shear is irretrievable from
outside — but bound (level-2) information remains intact on
whichever side it sits.  This is a sharper version of the
information-paradox answer than either "information is lost"
or "encoded in Hawking radiation alone."

**Hestenes / zitterbewegung (Q121 link).**  Q121 reads the
Born-rule randomness as ignorance of the instantaneous phase
of an *already-existing* Ma circulation.  The current question
is one level upstream: it asks how the Ma circulation got there
in the first place.  The two are complementary — Q121 takes
the particle as given; Q135 asks why measurement yields *any*
particle at all.

## 5. Computable handles

These are the falsification levers that make the question
finishable rather than purely interpretational.

**5.1. Landauer cost as level-1 → level-2 binding energy.**
The energy released when free information promotes to a bound
mode should equal the binding energy of the smallest
topological defect at level 2 — the minimal A6 vortex.  In
natural units (`κ = 1/(4πα) ≈ 1722`), the vortex core energy
is a calculable multiple of α and the relevant Compton scale.
Compare this to `kT ln 2` at the appropriate environmental
scale.  Order-of-magnitude agreement (modulo log factors) is a
non-trivial cross-check; many-orders-of-magnitude disagreement
falsifies the direct identification.

**5.2. Holevo capacity from ζ.**  The maximum information
flux through any Planck-area patch is bounded by `ζ × c` per
cell.  With ζ = 1/4 (A5), this gives a hard cap on the
classical information rate per photon mode that should match
the Holevo bound in the appropriate semi-classical limit.
A clean derivation closes the loop between A5 (information
resolution) and quantum-channel-capacity orthodoxy.

**5.3. Decoherence rate as vortex-formation rate.**  The
environmental decoherence rate γ_dec for a quantum system in
a thermal bath is empirically well-characterized
(γ_dec ∝ kT × γ_scattering at high temperature in Zurek's
treatment).  In the present picture, γ_dec should equal the
rate of A6 vortex-formation events per unit time on the hex
lattice under the same conditions.  A `sim-maxwell`-style
simulation of vortex creation rate vs. ambient photon density
gives a direct comparison target.

**5.4. Black-hole entropy budget split.**  An A5 horizon of
area A carries entropy `S = A/4`.  Of that, how much is
attributable to free (level-1) information versus promoted
(level-2) information?  The split should be derivable from
the hex-lattice shear geometry once that geometry is fully
worked out.  If the level-2 contribution dominates near
extremality (small horizons, near-Planck scale) and the
level-1 contribution dominates for large astrophysical
horizons, that matches the intuition that near-Planck systems
are highly bound while large systems are mostly thermal
radiation.

## 6. Open issues / what would falsify

- **Tensor-character mismatch with Q131.**  Q131's level-0
  residue (dark energy) is structurally featureless, hence
  `w = −1`.  The level-1 residue rides on photons and carries
  spin-1 tensor character.  The two residues are not the same
  *kind* of thing, so the architectural symmetry is partial,
  not exact.  This is consistent with the picture but worth
  flagging — the failed-promotion residue inherits whatever
  structure its substrate had.

- **What counts as "Ma-T² closure"?**  Decoherence in
  practice happens through countless mechanisms: scattering
  off thermal phonons, photon emission, gravitational
  coupling, etc.  The proposal needs a description of which
  of these qualify as level-2 promotion events.  A clean
  classification (e.g., "any process that binds a photon into
  a standing-wave configuration on at least one Ma sheet")
  would make the proposal more predictive.

- **Does the Born rule come out right numerically?**  Q121
  proposes phase-ignorance over Ma circulation as the source
  of Born probabilities.  The present question is consistent
  with Q121 but does not, by itself, recover the full
  quadratic form of `|ψ|²`.  Whether the Ma-T² closure
  condition naturally produces a quadratic phase distribution
  is a separate computational question.

- **Reversibility.**  De-promotion (forgetting / erasure) and
  promotion (measurement) should be topologically symmetric
  events.  But measurement is empirically irreversible while
  promotion-by-resonance is in principle reversible.  The
  asymmetry must come from the environment (entropy export),
  not from the topology — consistent with the standard
  decoherence story but worth stating explicitly.

## 7. Implications if true

- **The wavefunction is physical, not epistemic.**  ψ is
  literal level-1 information; collapse is a literal
  topological binding event.  This places the framework
  alongside Hestenes / zitterbewegung / pilot-wave traditions
  and against strict Copenhagen.
- **Measurement has an unavoidable thermodynamic cost.**
  Each promotion event releases binding energy.  Consistent
  with Landauer and provides a concrete mechanism for it.
- **Information has a hard bandwidth cap of `c × ζ` per
  cell.**  Free-information propagation cannot exceed photon
  flux.  Faster-than-light information transfer is forbidden
  not just by relativity but by lattice resolution.
- **Information is conserved across promotion / de-promotion
  events.**  Both are topological closures on a flat manifold.
  This places GRID firmly in the "information is conserved"
  camp on the black-hole-information question — consistent
  with Maldacena, Susskind, and current orthodoxy, but with
  a concrete lattice mechanism behind it.

## 8. Path forward

The Landauer cross-check (§5.1) is the smallest finishable
piece and the highest-leverage falsification handle: a
several-page calculation comparing the A6 vortex core energy
in natural units to `kT ln 2` at the appropriate temperature
scale.  If the answer matches, write it up as a Q-file
addendum and flag this question as partially answered.  If it
fails by many orders of magnitude, the level-1 → level-2
promotion identification needs revision (perhaps measurement
involves a lower-energy closure than the minimal vortex).

The other handles (Holevo bound from ζ, decoherence rate as
vortex rate, BH entropy split) are larger and more
speculative; they should wait until §5.1 is settled.
