## 1. The question

In MaSt, every massive particle is a photon captured on the
compact T⁶ manifold (Ma).  The photon circulates at speed c
on the torus — that's the standing wave.  Its frequency is
the particle's mass: E = hf = mc².

If the particle also moves through 3D space (S) at velocity v,
the photon's total speed is still c (it's a photon).  The
velocity must be SHARED between Ma circulation and S translation:

> v²_Ma + v²_S = c²

What happens to the torus when velocity is diverted to S?
Does MaSt offer insight into relativistic effects that standard
physics describes but doesn't explain mechanistically?

## 2. The velocity partition

A particle at rest: all velocity goes to Ma circulation.

> v_Ma = c,  v_S = 0

A particle moving at velocity v in S:

> v_S = v
> v_Ma = c × √(1 − v²/c²) = c/γ

where γ = 1/√(1 − v²/c²) is the Lorentz factor.

The photon's velocity budget is finite and fixed at c.
Moving in S leaves less for Ma.  This has consequences.

## 3. Time dilation: the light clock becomes real

**Standard physics:** a moving clock ticks slower by factor γ.
This is derived from the geometry of spacetime (Minkowski metric)
and demonstrated by the "light clock" thought experiment — a
photon bouncing between two mirrors takes longer per round trip
when the mirrors are moving.

**MaSt:** the particle IS a light clock.  The photon circulating
on the torus IS the clock.  Its tick rate is the Ma circulation
frequency:

> f_rest = mc²/h

When the particle moves at velocity v, the photon has less
Ma velocity (c/γ), so it takes longer per circuit:

> f_moving = f_rest / γ

This IS time dilation.  The "light clock thought experiment"
that Einstein used as a pedagogical tool is, in MaSt, the
literal physical mechanism.  The particle's internal clock
slows because the photon that IS the particle has less velocity
available for internal circulation.

Every particle is a light clock.  Time dilation is not an
abstract geometric property of spacetime — it's a photon
running out of velocity budget.

## 4. The energy-momentum relation: a right triangle

**Standard physics:** E² = (mc²)² + (pc)².  Derived from the
Lorentz group (a symmetry argument).

**MaSt:** this is the Pythagorean theorem on the photon's
velocity components:

> c² = v²_Ma + v²_S

Multiply both sides by (total photon energy / c)²:

> (E_total)² = (E_Ma)² + (E_S)²

where:
- E_Ma = mc² × (v_Ma/c) × γ = mc² (the rest energy)
- E_S = pc (the momentum contribution)

The famous energy-momentum relation is LITERALLY a right
triangle: the hypotenuse is c (total photon speed), one leg
is v_Ma (giving rest mass), the other leg is v_S (giving
momentum).

Standard physics says "this follows from Lorentz symmetry."
MaSt says "this follows from the geometry of a photon
splitting its velocity between two domains."  Both give the
same formula.  MaSt gives the physical picture.

## 5. Lorentz contraction: the torus compresses

When the particle moves at velocity v in the x-direction:

- The torus dimension parallel to x contracts by 1/γ
- Perpendicular dimensions are unchanged

In MaSt: the standing wave pattern on the torus is compressed
in the direction of motion because the photon's Ma-component
velocity is slower (c/γ), but the spatial translation is fast.
The wave crests pile up in the direction of motion and spread
out perpendicular to it.

This is standard Lorentz contraction, but with a mechanism:
the photon's wavefronts on the torus are carried along by the
S-velocity, squeezing the pattern in the direction of travel.

## 6. Massless particles: all velocity in S

A photon in free space has no Ma circulation — it doesn't
wind around any torus.  All its velocity is in S:

> v_Ma = 0,  v_S = c

It has zero rest mass (no Ma frequency), travels at exactly c
(all velocity in S), and cannot be slowed down (there's no Ma
component to transfer velocity TO).

In MaSt, the distinction between massive and massless is:

> **Massive:** photon captured on Ma (some velocity in Ma, some in S)
> **Massless:** photon free in S (all velocity in S)

A massive particle can never reach v = c in S because it can
never fully stop its Ma circulation.  There's always some
frequency, some rest mass, some velocity budget used internally.
To reach c would mean stopping the internal clock — the
standing wave pattern would collapse and the particle would
cease to exist as a bound mode.

## 7. Approaching the speed of light

As v → c:

| Quantity | Behavior | MaSt mechanism |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| v_Ma | → 0 | photon has no velocity left for Ma |
| Frequency | → 0 | torus clock freezes |
| Time dilation | → ∞ | internal clock stops |
| Lorentz contraction | → 0 | torus flattens to a disc |
| Kinetic energy | → ∞ | energy goes to S momentum, not Ma |
| Rest mass | unchanged | Ma circulation rate drops but the MODE persists |

The particle's "internal life" stops at v = c.  No internal
transitions, no decays, no clock ticks.  This is exactly what
is observed: unstable particles at high velocity live longer
(muon lifetime at high speed is γ × 2.2 μs).

In MaSt, this IS the mechanism for cosmic ray muon survival.
The muon's Ma clock runs slower when the muon moves fast,
because the photon inside it has less velocity available for
Ma circulation.  The muon doesn't decay because its internal
transitions are frozen — the photon can't complete the
rearrangement needed for decay.

## 8. Does MaSt change any predictions?

**No.** Every formula — time dilation, Lorentz contraction,
E² = m²c⁴ + p²c², muon lifetime extension — is identical to
standard special relativity.  MaSt adds no corrections, no
new terms, no deviations.

What MaSt adds is a **mechanism**: why these formulas work.
Standard physics says "spacetime has Minkowski geometry."
MaSt says "photons have a fixed velocity budget shared between
internal circulation and spatial translation."

Both are valid descriptions.  They may be equivalent.  But
the MaSt picture offers a tactile, mechanical intuition:

- Time slows because the clock is a photon and the photon is busy elsewhere
- E² = m²c⁴ + p²c² because it's a right triangle
- Massless particles go at c because they have nothing internal
- Massive particles can't reach c because they can't stop being a clock

## 9. What this means for the project

The velocity partition between Ma and S is not a new prediction
— it's a reinterpretation of special relativity.  It doesn't
generate testable differences from standard physics.

However, it confirms that the MaSt framework is CONSISTENT
with special relativity.  The compact Ma dimensions and the
spatial S dimensions share the photon's velocity in exactly
the way that produces the known Lorentz group.  This is a
non-trivial consistency check: a model that claims particles
are photons on a compact space MUST reproduce special relativity,
and it does.

The deeper implication: if the particle IS the photon, and the
photon's velocity partition IS special relativity, then special
relativity is not a postulate — it's a CONSEQUENCE of the
photon being the fundamental object.  The speed of light is
the speed limit because the speed of light is the speed of
everything — particles just divide it between internal and
external motion.
