## 1. The question

MaSt/GRID's architecture is a three-layer promotion ladder
(photon-from-aleph §"information promotion"):

- **Level 0** — raw phase information on the lattice.
- **Level 1** — the ℵ-line (S¹) compactification promotes
  that information into a U(1) 1-form whose KK zero mode is
  the photon (spin 1).
- **Level 2** — each Ma sheet (T²) compactification promotes
  a photon into a Dirac–Kähler fermion via KK reduction
  (spin ½).

At each level, compactification picks out a **resonance
condition** — integer winding along each compact cycle —
which determines which lower-level content is promoted to
the next layer.

The question:  **what happens to content that fails the
resonance condition at a given layer?**  Q125 gives one
concrete answer at the sheet layer: content that forms a
T⁶ compound but fails the spin-alignment condition for α
coupling exists as uncharged mass — i.e., dark matter.
Does the same principle applied at the aleph layer give a
structural account of dark energy?

## 2. The proposal

**Dark energy as unpromoted lattice information.**

> Raw phase information on GRID's lattice that satisfies
> the aleph-S¹ closure condition (integer compact
> momentum) promotes to a photon (massless if `n = 0`,
> massive KK excitation if `n ≥ 1`).  Information that
> does NOT satisfy this closure condition cannot form a
> 1-form KK mode — it remains as level-0 content:
> structureless phase fluctuation distributed across the
> lattice.  This residue carries energy density but has no
> Lorentz-tensor character, no particle identity, no
> directional flow, and no peculiar velocity.  Its
> observable manifestation in the 4D effective theory is a
> vacuum-like energy density with equation of state
> `w = −1`: the cosmological constant / dark energy.

This is a one-line extension of Q125's architectural
principle, applied one level lower.

The pattern is uniform:  at every layer, resonance promotes
and non-resonance either remains at the layer below or
appears as a structureless background at the layer above.

## 4. Why the equation of state comes out right

Dark energy's defining property is `w ≈ −1`: energy
density that doesn't dilute with cosmic expansion (`ρ ∝
a⁰` rather than `ρ ∝ a⁻³` for matter or `a⁻⁴` for
radiation).  Any candidate mechanism has to source this
value for a structural reason, not by tuning.

Under this proposal, the reason is direct:

- Unpromoted information is a **property of the lattice
  state**, not a gas of excitations.
- It is not counted per-particle — there is no particle.
- There is no volume-integrated conserved number whose
  dilution would track with expansion.
- Its contribution to the effective 4D stress-energy is
  therefore uniform in space and constant in time at
  leading order, which is exactly the cosmological-constant
  form of `T_μν ∝ g_μν` giving `w = −1`.

This is the same argument that gives Casimir / vacuum
energy `w = −1` in standard QFT, but it is sourced
specifically from MaSt/GRID's architecture:  the residue
of a level-0 content that didn't make it through the
level-1 promotion filter, not a sum of zero-point modes
tacked on by hand.

## 5. Parallel to Q125 — two "darks" with the same
structural role

The two dark sectors of the Standard Model of cosmology now
have parallel architectural origins:

| Phenomenology | MaSt/GRID origin | Mechanism | Layer that failed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dark matter | Q125 | T⁶ mode formed but spin-alignment fails → α coupling = 0 | sheet layer |
| Dark energy | Q131 (this file) | Lattice information fails aleph-closure → no photon; residue is structureless | aleph layer |

The unifying statement:

> **Content that is geometrically present but fails its
> layer's promotion condition appears at the next layer as
> a structureless background.  The specific signature
> (clustering matter vs. vacuum-like energy) is determined
> by which layer the failure occurred at.**

Failure at the sheet layer means the content still had
mass (it formed a T⁶ compound) but lacked the
spin-alignment needed for α coupling — it gravitates and
clusters, i.e. cold dark matter.  Failure at the aleph
layer means the content never acquired particle identity
at all — it's distributed phase energy, i.e. vacuum-like.

## 6. Relation to the "heavy KK photon" alternative

A separate candidate for dark content from aleph is the
**integer-winding KK tower** (`n ≥ 1` modes of the 1-form
on S¹).  Those are genuine eigenstates — they *are*
resonant — and they carry energy as massive vector
particles at `m = n ℏc / R_ℵ`.

They are not the dark-energy candidate here:

- They are resonant, not the residue of failed resonance.
- They have `w = 0` (non-relativistic matter) or `w = 1/3`
  (relativistic), diluting with expansion.
- They cluster under gravity.

If present, they would contribute an additional
**heavy-dark-matter** component, parallel to Q125's
sheet-layer dark matter.  They do not explain the
accelerating expansion.

Q131's dark energy comes specifically from the
**non-resonant** sector — information that never became a
particle at all.

## 7. What would need to be derived to make this rigorous

1. **Operational definition of "non-resonant information"
   on GRID's lattice.**  Presumably the part of the U(1)
   phase field on ℵ whose Fourier decomposition along the
   compact direction is not integer-peaked.  Writing this
   precisely would make the residue energy density a
   concrete integral over the non-integer part of the
   spectrum.

2. **Coupling to gravity.**  Why does the residue source a
   `T_μν` that looks like `ρ g_μν` in the 4D effective
   theory?  Almost certainly follows from the lattice
   thermodynamics chain in `grid/synthesis.md` /
   `grid/gravity.md`, but needs to be spelled out.

3. **The cosmological-constant magnitude.**  The naive
   Casimir estimate for compact S¹ gives vacuum energy of
   order `ℏc / L_ℵ⁴` — Planck density if `L_ℵ ≈ ℓ_P`, i.e.
   ~10¹²³ times the observed dark-energy density.  The
   cosmological-constant problem wears a new outfit under
   this proposal but does not go away:  *why does the
   non-resonant residue renormalize to the observed Λ?*
   A successful derivation would either show a structural
   cancellation that leaves a small remainder, or identify
   a separate length scale (not `ℓ_P`) that sets Λ.

4. **Compatibility with Q130.**  Q130 considers
   non-standing photons in T⁶ as a separate bath with its
   own equation of state.  Q131 considers unpromoted
   lattice information as a parallel bath at the aleph
   level.  Are they independent, additive, or does one
   subsume the other?  The two should be developed
   together.

5. **Verification of the matter-analog row.**  Row 2 of
   the table (light → matter on sheet) claims that
   non-resonant light on a sheet stays as free radiation.
   This is the observed behavior — unbound photons
   propagate rather than confine — but the parallel with
   rows 1 and 3 should be made explicit so the promotion
   principle is uniformly stated across all layers.

## 8. Predictions and consequences

If the proposal is correct:

- **Dark energy density is determined by the lattice's
  non-resonant spectrum.**  In principle computable once
  GRID's lattice parameters are fixed (see item 3 above).
- **Dark energy does not fluctuate at particle scales.**
  No "dark energy quanta" to detect in collider or
  astrophysical searches — it's a property of the vacuum
  state, not a particle species.
- **Possible time dependence from lattice evolution.**  If
  the non-resonant fraction of information shifts over
  cosmological time (e.g., because the lattice itself
  evolves thermodynamically per GRID's gravity treatment),
  `w` could deviate slightly from `−1` in ways that
  current/future dark-energy surveys (DESI, Roman, Euclid)
  can bound.  This is a concrete observational handle.
- **Correlation between α and Λ.**  Both are expressions
  of the aleph geometry (α from GRID's derivation in R31
  etc.; Λ from this proposal).  If both ultimately trace
  to the same parameter(s) of `L_ℵ`, there is a structural
  relationship between them that is absent in standard
  physics.  Testable in principle.

## 9. Relation to existing physics

### 9.1 — The information-below-energy ontology has substantial standard backing

Q131 takes for granted that **information sits below
energy in the ontological stack** — energy is what happens
when information gets promoted through a compact-topology
filter.  This directional claim is not idiosyncratic to
MaSt/GRID; it is the dominant direction of theoretical
physics over the last ~30 years, supported by a chain of
well-established results:

- **Landauer's principle (1961).**  Erasing one bit
  dissipates at least `kT · ln 2` of energy as heat.
  Information is the primitive being manipulated; energy
  is the cost of the manipulation.  The arrow runs from
  bits to energy, not the other way.
- **Bekenstein bound (1973, 1981).**  `I ≤ 2πkRE/(ℏc · ln 2)`
  — the information capacity of a region is bounded by
  its energy and radius.  Energy *bounds* information,
  consistent with information being the underlying
  quantity and energy being the enabler.
- **Bekenstein–Hawking entropy.**  A black hole's entropy
  is `A/(4ℓ_P²)` — a count of information per Planck area.
  Spacetime regions have informational content set by
  their geometry, not their matter content.
- **Holographic principle** ('t Hooft 1993, Susskind 1995).
  All physics in a bulk volume is encoded on its boundary
  at ~1 bit per Planck area.  The bulk — including its
  energy, fields, and metric — emerges from the
  boundary's information.
- **AdS/CFT** (Maldacena 1997).  The bulk gravitational
  theory is *dual to* a boundary conformal field theory.
  The boundary is informational; bulk energy and geometry
  are derived.
- **Wheeler's "It from Bit"** (1989).  The foundational
  statement: every physical entity derives its existence
  from binary informational answers.  Q131's promotion
  hierarchy is directly in this lineage.
- **Lloyd's computational universe.**  Energy is what
  computation *does*, not what it *is*; physical processes
  are computations on quantum information.
- **Jacobson (1995) / Verlinde (2011) entropic gravity.**
  Einstein equations derived from thermodynamic /
  information-counting principles at local horizons; in
  Verlinde's later work, dark energy itself is read as an
  information-theoretic property of the cosmological
  horizon.

The institutional expression of this view is the *It from
Qubit* collaboration in current quantum-gravity research.
None of these results uniquely entail Q131's specific
mechanism, but together they license the framing:
**information is the layer below energy, and the question
"what does unpromoted information look like at the next
layer up?" is a legitimate one to ask within the
information-first program.**

### 9.2 — How Q131 sits next to specific physics pictures

This proposal sits next to several standard pictures
without duplicating any of them:

- **Casimir energy from compact dimensions** (standard KK
  literature).  The resemblance is structural: both give
  `w = −1` from a compact-dim vacuum effect.  The
  difference is that Casimir computes zero-point sums over
  *integer* modes; Q131 sources the energy from
  *non*-integer (non-resonant) spectral content.  These
  are different subsets of the full phase-field content on
  ℵ and are in principle distinguishable.
- **Quintessence / scalar dark energy.**  Q131 is not a
  scalar field; it's a non-particle residue.  No equation
  of motion, no slow-roll dynamics — `w = −1` from the
  structural argument in §4, not from a potential.
- **Holographic / emergent dark energy** (Verlinde-style).
  Closest in spirit: both read dark energy as a
  thermodynamic / informational property of the underlying
  substrate.  Q131 is more specific — it identifies
  *which* substrate content sources it (non-resonant
  aleph information) rather than appealing to a generic
  horizon-entropy argument.

### 9.3 — What Q131 adds beyond standard info-first physics

The standard info-first program supports the
*directionality* of Q131's hierarchy (information below
energy) but does not specify either of the two pieces that
make Q131 a concrete proposal:

1. **The promotion mechanism** — compactification
   resonance on the ℵ-line as the specific filter that
   selects which level-0 information becomes a photon.
   Standard info-first physics is silent on the mechanism;
   it just says information is fundamental.
2. **The residue's manifestation** — that the
   *un*promoted fraction is what we observe as dark
   energy with `w = −1`.  Verlinde gestures at
   information-as-dark-energy but through a horizon
   argument, not through a compactification filter.

Both are MaSt/GRID-specific claims that extend the
mainstream program rather than following from it.  This is
where Q131's structural content lives, and where its
derivation work needs to focus.
