# Q63. Why is the electron confined to a 2D plane on T³?

**Status:** Answered — R19 F31
**Source:** R19 Track 4/5 discussion
**Connects to:** R14 F2, R19, Q13
**Model era:** Model A/B

R14 F2 assumed the electron uses 2 of 3 material dimensions with the third "inert." This was never derived.

Answered by R19 F31 — the 3D charge integral on sheared T³ gives a selection rule: s₁₃ = 0 (no shear in the third dimension) kills charge for any mode with n₃ ≠ 0. The electron must have n₃ = 0, confining it to a 2D plane. Additionally, (1, 2, 0) has shorter geodesic length than (1, 2, k) for k ≠ 0, making it the lowest-energy charged spin-½ state. Both energetic and charge-based arguments agree: the electron is planar on T³.
