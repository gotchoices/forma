# Q43. What selects the harmonic number q? (Wave-language α problem)

**Status:** Open — wave reframing of the α problem; q is free, ~100–200
**Source:** User question
**Model era:** Model A (wave-language series Q41–Q46)

A material-space cavity supports all harmonics in principle. The electron corresponds to some harmonic n = q, where q is in the range ~100–200. Lower harmonics would be higher-energy bound states; higher harmonics lower-energy ones. What physical principle selects one particular q as the stable ground state? Candidates: energy minimization within a topological sector, self-consistency of the field (the mode must be its own source), or boundary conditions imposed by the coupling to 3D. This is the wave-language restatement of Q18 (what selects q?). Does framing it as a cavity eigenvalue problem — find the self-consistent mode — make it more tractable than the particle-path version?
