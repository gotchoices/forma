What is the shape of matter?

Forma is an open exploration of whether all matter and forces might arise from energy and geometry.

The central idea is simple: if general relativity explains gravity as the shape of spacetime, perhaps electromagnetism can also be understood in a similar way.

The short version

Physics spent centuries breaking the world down into smaller and smaller pieces. At a certain point, the pieces stopped looking like little balls and started looking more like waves. Forma takes that view seriously.

In this picture there is only energy (light) and geometry (the rules of how light moves). Mass appears when light is confined in a certain way. Charge (electricity) is not a separate ingredient, but emerges from a further layer of confinement.

This offers a simpler way to think about the fundamentals: fewer starting assumptions, more properties that emerge.

Why it matters

General relativity changed gravity from a force into a shape. Forma asks whether electromagnetism works the same way.
