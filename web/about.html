About

Who, why, and how to judge what you’re reading.

What Forma is

Forma is an open exploration of whether the particles, charges, and long-range forces of nature might be geometric consequences of a very small number of fundamentals: light (energy) and geometry (a discrete lattice with a few compact extra dimensions).

The formal project name in the repository is GRID + MaSt — two stacked layers. Forma (Latin: shape, form) is the public face, and formares.org ("the shape of things") is the home page. The underlying work lives on GitHub at github.com/gotchoices/forma.

Who is behind this

The author is an electrical engineer, not a trained physicist. The project started as a hobby — a what if exploration of a 1997 paper (Williamson and van der Mark) that proposed the electron is a confined photon. It has since produced results the author did not expect.

Forma is a project of GotChoices.org.

What AI did and didn’t do

The author has served as chief strategist over the project: choosing which ideas to pursue, deciding when a theory doesn’t pan out, and presenting the next round of hypotheses for testing. The research, computational modeling, mathematical derivations, and literature comparisons have been performed with substantial help from AI (Claude). The author’s job has been to keep the agents focused and honest; there has admittedly been a certain amount of slop generated along the way, and the project is open about that.

What is claimed — and what isn’t

We do not claim that this model is correct. It may turn out to be a mathematical coincidence, a tautology, or an elaborate exercise in numerology. We expect only one out of 137 physicists to take a serious look at it.

What we do claim is that it is interesting: a model with a handful of experimental inputs that then produces percent-level mass accommodation for virtually all observed particles is, at minimum, worth understanding better — why it works as well as it does, and precisely where and why it fails.

How to judge reliability

Every numerical result is produced by scripts in the repository that can be inspected and re-run. That is what we hope the reader will do.

Content typeLocationReliability
Runnable computational studies studies/, grid/sim-*/ Highest — verify by running the scripts yourself
Theoretical derivations grid/maxwell.md, grid/gravity.md Solid — follows established physics (lattice gauge theory, Jacobson), but read critically
Q&A files and hypotheses qa/ Ranges from well-reasoned to speculative brainstorming

When in doubt, look for the script.

Openness and invitation

The project is conducted in the open. The studies, scripts, findings, and failures are all there. Readers with relevant expertise — in lattice gauge theory, Kaluza–Klein compactification, particle phenomenology, or neutrino physics — are genuinely welcome to examine, challenge, or extend the work.

The best way to engage is to clone the repository and run things for yourself. Issues, pull requests, and serious criticism are all welcome there.

Intellectual debts

Forma builds on a line of thought running through a century of physics:

  • de Broglie (1924) — particles are waves (λ = h/p).
  • Schrödinger (1926) — the wavefunction as a real physical wave, with zitterbewegung as a hint of underlying circular motion.
  • Kaluza (1921) & Klein (1926) — electromagnetism as geometry, via one compact extra dimension.
  • Williamson & van der Mark (1997) — the specific proposal that an electron is a photon confined to a (1, 2) torus knot.

Forma extends these threads into a full 11-dimensional architecture with three material sheets, a discrete substrate, and per-sheet Dirac–Kähler spin. Whether the synthesis holds up is the question the project exists to answer.
