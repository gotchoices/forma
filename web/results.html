What the geometry produces

Particle spectrum

Under a Z₃-compliant + composite-α mode search, 14 of 16 non-input compound particles land within 1.12% of observed mass (several beating model-E). The three charged lepton generations (electron, muon, tau) come out with algebraically exact mass ratios from a single geometric solution point.

Nuclei

Using a single scaling law n_pt = 3A, n_pr = 6A, n_et = 1 − Z, nuclear masses from deuterium to ⁵⁶Fe land within 1.4%. The Coulomb self-energy of a Z-nucleus scales as exactly Z²·α — not imposed, but forced by the tube↔ℵ↔t coupling chain.

Neutrinos

The ratio of squared mass splittings Δm²₃₁ / Δm²₂₁ ≈ 33.6 is produced analytically from integer winding numbers on the neutrino sheet, with a unique shear s_ν = 0.022 fixing it. Neutrino neutrality is derived, not postulated: real-field KK modes are tube-conjugate symmetric, and absent a symmetry-breaker (which the electron and proton sheets have, but the neutrino sheet does not) the tube charge averages to zero automatically.

Quantum numbers

  • Charge quantization from periodicity of the phase winding. No fractional charges possible on a closed surface.
  • Spin ½ from the (1, 2) winding ratio on a flat 2-torus sheet — not postulated, topological.
  • Electron shell structure (2n²) from the mode count of confined-wave solutions on the compact geometry.
  • Standard Model taxonomy (lepton / meson / baryon ↔ 1-, 2-, 3-sheet) from sheet-count + SU(2) composition.

Dark matter as a prediction, not a patch

Most modes on the material sheets have even winding numbers. Their fields oscillate symmetrically, producing zero net charge. They carry mass (standing-wave energy) but couple weakly to light — the exact behavior observed in dark matter. Below 2 GeV there are roughly 900 such modes, outnumbering charged particles ~20 : 1. The predicted mass ratio brackets the observed dark-to-visible ratio of ~5.4.

Emergent neutron

The neutron was not put in by hand. It emerges as a cross-sheet compound mode spanning the proton and electron sheets, with exactly zero net charge — a prediction, not an input.

Z₃ confinement on the proton sheet

The (3, 6) proton is a bound state of three (1, 2) sub-modes — the framework’s version of quarks. Their confinement is geometric: the individual constituents fall below the sheet’s resonance threshold and cannot survive independently. Confinement is not imposed; it is a consequence of the geometry.

What remains open

The project is conducted in the open. These are listed in the repository, not hidden. Anyone with relevant expertise is welcome to examine, challenge, or extend the work.

  • Pion mass desert. π⁰ and π± land ~10–13% below observed; halved from earlier models but not closed.
  • The value of α. Its physical meaning has become clearer (an impedance coupling between compact and extended space), but its specific value, 1/137.036, must still be taken from experiment.
  • Single-k closed form. k = 1.1803/(8π) is a confirmed structural fixed point across all three sheets, but a closed-form derivation of its specific value is open.
  • Flavor (u/d split). Not yet derived within the (1,2)-quark / (3,6)-proton architecture.
  • Matter–antimatter asymmetry. A plausible shear-chirality sketch exists; formalization is pending.
  • Gauge-boson sector. W / Z as transient reconfigurations is a partial picture; sin² θ_W ≈ 3/13 has a provisional derivation.
  • Photon-on-GRID, rigorous derivation. A planned companion to the per-sheet Dirac–Kähler derivation.
