Three entry paths — pick the one that matches how you like to learn.

Forma approaches the same subject — the geometric structure of matter and spacetime — from different directions, for different audiences. Not everyone enters through the same door. Pick the path that suits you; they converge.

All of the material below lives in the open Forma repository on GitHub. These links point there directly.

The scientific path for the technically inclined

Start here if you think in equations, simulations, and testable predictions.

  1. What If?

    The method. How dropping one assumption — matter is stuff — leads to a geometric model with one free parameter. No equations.

  2. Good Unification Theory

    The arc, in one read. Where the framework sits alongside Einstein, Kaluza, and the Standard Model: what it unifies, what it leaves open, and why Good instead of Grand. ~10 minutes.

  3. Matter from Light — A Geometric Particle Spectrum

    The white paper. Full model spectrum, T⁶ geometry, three generations from shear resonance, nuclear scaling, and open problems.

  4. Derivations — From Photon to Particle

    The formal foundation. Roadmap and index for the analytical derivations that prove the framework’s postulates from special relativity + E = hf + compact geometry.

  5. Matter from Light

    The particle model in narrative form. How confined photons on compact geometry produce mass, charge, spin, and the particle spectrum.

  6. The Ghost in the Machine

    Dark matter as the uncharged majority of the same standing waves that produce the visible particles.

For the lattice foundations beneath the particle model, see the primers (especially physics-from-fabric and alpha-in-grid) and the GRID technical documents.

The intuitive path for the pattern-minded

Start here if you think in patterns, symbols, and complementary structures.

  1. A Light Descent into Symbols

    The duality: yin and yang, 1 and 0, E and B, delta and wye — the same complementary pattern at every scale, from the lattice to the constants.

  2. Little Balls of Light

    The story. From the Greeks to the Standard Model, humanity has been looking for smaller and smaller pieces of stuff. What if the pieces are light?

  3. What If?

    The method. The scientific and intuitive paths both meet here.

The reflective path for the contemplative

If you are drawn to the intersection of geometry, meaning, and ancient intuition. These pieces are personal reflections, not scientific claims. They arise from the same project but do not depend on it — and the science does not depend on them.

  1. 137: The Divine Signature

    Is the fine-structure constant an artistic signature encoded in both physics and scripture?

  2. Ancient Geometric Structure

    The Genesis creation sequence and the GRID + MaSt coordinate hierarchy: structural correlations, honestly assessed.

  3. A Light Descent into Symbols

    A symbolic reading of the lattice geometry and the number 137.036.
