# KK Charge Comparison  *(concluded)*

Does WvM's charge derivation relate to the Kaluza-Klein charge
mechanism? If so, how? If not, what framework should we use?

## Result

**No.** The KK gravitational charge mechanism (charge = compact
momentum × √G coupling) operates at the Planck scale (~10⁻³⁴ m)
and produces charges of ~10⁻²² × e at the Compton scale where
WvM structures live. The WvM charge mechanism (electromagnetic
field topology) is structurally different and does not involve G.

The charge question itself was already resolved by S2 (toroid-
geometry): a/R = 1/√(πα) gives q = e exactly. This study
confirms that the resolution is WvM's own, not a KK result.

The compact-dimension topology (T²) remains useful for
confinement (geodesic, no force), spin (double winding), and
mass quantization — but charge comes from the photon's field
profile, not from compact momentum.

## Key Findings

1. 6D metric decomposes into 4D gravity + 2 gauge fields (U(1) ×
   U(1)) + 3 scalars (F1–F3).
2. KK gravitational charges are ~10⁻²² × e at Compton-scale
   compact radii (F5–F6).
3. WvM charge (with S2's geometry) gives q = e without G (F7–F8).
4. Compact topology provides confinement, spin, mass — not
   charge (F9).
5. The open question is deriving a/R = 1/√(πα) from first
   principles (F12).

## Retrospective

This study was framed as "should WvM reduce to KK?" — trying
to force the WvM model into the KK framework. The answer is no,
and that answer was partially available from S2 already (WvM
gives q = e with the right geometry; no need for KK's charge
mechanism). The useful output is the 6D decomposition and the
definitive scale separation (10²¹×) between KK and WvM.
