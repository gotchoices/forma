# Review: Standard Model Particles

Quick reference for the knot zoo study. Only the properties relevant
to particle identification (spin, charge, mass, composition) are
covered here. This is not a full Standard Model review.

---

## 1. Fermions vs Bosons

| | Fermions | Bosons |
|---|---|---|
| Spin | Half-integer (½, 3/2, ...) | Integer (0, 1, 2) |
| Statistics | Fermi-Dirac (no two can share a quantum state) | Bose-Einstein (can pile into the same state) |
| Role | Matter | Forces (and Higgs) |
| Examples | Electron, quarks, neutrinos | Photon, W/Z, gluon, Higgs |

---

## 2. The Matter Particles (Fermions)

### Leptons — do not feel the strong force

| Particle | Charge | Mass (MeV/c²) | Mass ratio to e |
|----------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Electron (e) | -1 | 0.511 | 1 |
| Muon (μ) | -1 | 105.7 | 206.8 |
| Tau (τ) | -1 | 1777 | 3477 |
| Electron neutrino (ν_e) | 0 | < 0.0001 | ~0 |
| Muon neutrino (ν_μ) | 0 | < 0.2 | ~0 |
| Tau neutrino (ν_τ) | 0 | < 18 | ~0 |

Each charged lepton has a neutrino partner. All have spin ½.
Each has an antiparticle with opposite charge (positron e⁺, etc.).

### Quarks — feel the strong force, carry "color" charge

| Particle | Charge | Mass (MeV/c²) | Mass ratio to e | Generation |
|----------|--------|---------------|-----------------|------------|
| Up (u) | +2/3 | ~2.2 | 4.3 | 1st |
| Down (d) | -1/3 | ~4.7 | 9.2 | 1st |
| Charm (c) | +2/3 | ~1275 | 2495 | 2nd |
| Strange (s) | -1/3 | ~95 | 186 | 2nd |
| Top (t) | +2/3 | ~173000 | 338600 | 3rd |
| Bottom (b) | -1/3 | ~4180 | 8180 | 3rd |

All quarks have spin ½. They come in three "colors" (red, green, blue)
and are never observed free — always confined in hadrons. Each has an
antiquark with opposite charge and color.

**Pattern:** Charges come in two values: +2/3 and -1/3. Each generation
has one of each. The three generations differ only in mass.

## 3. Force Carriers (Bosons)

| Particle | Spin | Charge | Mass (GeV/c²) | Force |
|----------|------|--------|--------------|-------|
| Photon (γ) | 1 | 0 | 0 | Electromagnetic |
| W⁺, W⁻ | 1 | ±1 | 80.4 | Weak |
| Z⁰ | 1 | 0 | 91.2 | Weak |
| Gluon (g) | 1 | 0 | 0 | Strong |
| Higgs (H) | 0 | 0 | 125 | Mass mechanism |
| Graviton | 2 | 0 | 0 | Gravity (hypothetical) |

The photon is massless and unconfined — in the WvM framework, it is
the fundamental building block, not a knot.

## 4. Composite Particles (Hadrons)

Quarks combine via the strong force into hadrons. Only "color-neutral"
combinations are allowed:

### Baryons (3 quarks) — fermions, spin ½ or 3/2

| Particle | Quarks | Charge | Mass (MeV/c²) | Stable? |
|----------|--------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Proton (p) | u u d | +2/3 +2/3 -1/3 = +1 | 938.3 | Yes |
| Neutron (n) | u d d | +2/3 -1/3 -1/3 = 0 | 939.6 | No (τ ≈ 15 min) |
| Delta⁺⁺ (Δ⁺⁺) | u u u | +2 | 1232 | No |
| Omega⁻ (Ω⁻) | s s s | -1 | 1672 | No |

### Mesons (quark + antiquark) — bosons, spin 0 or 1

| Particle | Quarks | Charge | Mass (MeV/c²) |
|----------|--------|--------|---------------|
| Pion⁺ (π⁺) | u d̄ | +1 | 139.6 |
| Pion⁰ (π⁰) | (uū - dd̄)/√2 | 0 | 135.0 |
| Kaon⁺ (K⁺) | u s̄ | +1 | 493.7 |

## 5. What You Need to Build All Stable Matter

Only **three particles** are required:

| Particle | Spin | Charge | Role |
|----------|------|--------|------|
| **Electron** (e) | ½ | -1 | Orbits nucleus |
| **Up quark** (u) | ½ | +2/3 | Constituent of proton (uud) and neutron (udd) |
| **Down quark** (d) | ½ | -1/3 | Constituent of proton (uud) and neutron (udd) |

Everything else — muons, taus, strange/charm/bottom/top quarks,
W/Z bosons — is unstable and decays. All stable atoms in the
universe are built from these three fermions plus the photon
(which mediates the electromagnetic force holding atoms together)
and gluons (which hold quarks together in protons and neutrons).

### Key constraints for knot candidates

All three are **spin-½ fermions** → q = 2 knots in our framework.

The charges are **-1, +2/3, -1/3**. The fractional quark charges
are a crucial test: can different (p,2) knots produce charges of
2/3 and 1/3? Or does the charge mechanism need modification?

Charge signs (positive vs negative) might come from chirality —
a left-handed vs right-handed knot.

## 6. Key Quantum Numbers (Summary)

| Quantum Number | Values | What it means |
|---------------|--------|---------------|
| Spin | 0, ½, 1, 3/2, 2 | Intrinsic angular momentum |
| Electric charge | -1, -1/3, 0, +2/3, +1 | Electromagnetic coupling |
| Baryon number | 0 (leptons), 1/3 (quarks) | Conserved in all interactions |
| Lepton number | 1 (leptons), 0 (quarks) | Conserved (approximately) |
| Color | r, g, b (quarks only) | Strong force charge |
| Flavor | u, d, s, c, b, t | Which quark type |
