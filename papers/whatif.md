Thoughts for what-if paper:

Make this a thought-provoking read for any audience.  Non-technical, philosophical but engaging.

- Throughout scientific history, certain discoveries started by someone asking "what if?"
- What if we've been thinking about this in the wrong way?
- What if I remove one of my primary assumptions?
- What if I remove _all_ of my assumptions?
- It is an exercise in open mindedness
- It defines the difference between science and belief
- Science is a systematic process of asking questions and testing assertions
- Belief is:
  - the set of axioms we accept at the onset, and
  - the interpretations we place on our observations
- Both science and belief are important
- We have to start with belief, otherwise how could we live (2 Corthinians 5:7)
- We simply can't _know_ everything.  We can know what we individually and personally prove/witness.
  But everything else, we have to apply a certain number of assumptions/beliefs
- Cite a few key points in scientific history where someone asked, what if
  - Non-euclidian geometry: Which axiom was tossed out?
  - Relativity: What axioms/beliefs dispensed
  - Maybe one or two other examples?
- MaSt began as a what-if project
  - Our belief is that matter is material (hence the name)
  - So our axioms have followed that belief
  - Big matter, big chunks, or balls of stuff (reference to little balls paper
  - Little matter, little balls (atoms)
    (Oh yea, and there's this thing called waves-a macro-phenomena of little balls)
  - littler matter, littler balls (standard model)
    Wait, there's this kind of wave that doesn't have little balls (EM, no aether, etc)
    And it looks like waves act like balls and balls act like waves
- Problem is, the smaller we get, the less stuff looks like balls and the more it looks like light
- What if there are not really any "little balls", but just light 
  (Admittedly a big jump--just of jettisoning our tether to reality here!)
- What if we just dropped the axiom that "balls" are a thing and jump right to the light-based model?
  What would happen?
  What would the model look like? (MaSt)
  Why might it be more correct (GRID, a deeper model, explaining the aether)
  What would it predict (perhaps the most important part)?
- What is the simplest set of axioms (i.e. beliefs we can achieve)
  IOW, minimize beliefs, maximize observations; Authentic Science
