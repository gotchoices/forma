<!--EC THIN OUTLINE — sequential arc for review

1. THE QUESTION
   - "What if?" — the most productive question in science
   - Not rebellion — discipline: willingness to test your own assumptions

2. SCIENCE AND BELIEF
   - We all start with beliefs (axioms we accept before testing)
   - Science is the process of questioning them systematically
   - Belief is necessary — we can't prove everything from scratch (2 Cor 5:7)
   - The goal: minimize beliefs, maximize what you derive from observation

3. HISTORICAL WHAT-IFS
   (one paragraph each: what was the axiom, who dropped it, what emerged)

   a. Euclid → non-Euclidean geometry
      Axiom dropped: parallel lines never meet
      Result: curved spaces; foundation for general relativity

   b. Newton → Einstein (special relativity)
      Axiom dropped: time is absolute
      Result: spacetime, E = mc², time dilation

   c. Newton → Einstein (general relativity)
      Axiom dropped: gravity is a force
      Result: gravity is geometry; curved spacetime

   d. Classical → quantum
      Axiom dropped: energy is continuous
      Result: discrete energy levels, wave-particle duality

   e. (Optional) Steady state → Big Bang
      Axiom dropped: the universe is eternal and unchanging
      Result: expansion, CMB, nucleosynthesis

   f. The pattern: every breakthrough removed an assumption everyone took for granted

4. THE MATTER ASSUMPTION — 2500 years of smaller balls
   - Greeks: earth, water, air, fire — chunks of stuff
   - Dalton: atoms — small balls
   - Thomson/Rutherford: electrons orbiting nuclei — smaller balls
   - Standard model: quarks, leptons — smallest balls

   - Particles: wave-particle duality (acts like both, is neither)
