## 1. Two sizes, one particle

- Hook: every particle carries two "sizes" — both set by the same number (its mass), yet they behave oppositely
- Schwarzschild radius r_s = 2Gm/c²
  - Gravitational size; grows with mass
  - Electron: r_s ≈ 10⁻⁵⁷ m — absurdly tiny
- Compton wavelength λ_C = h/(mc)
  - Quantum size; shrinks with mass
  - Electron: λ_C = 2.4 × 10⁻¹² m — forty-five orders of magnitude larger
- The crossover: they meet at exactly one mass — the Planck mass (22 μg, a grain of sand)
  - Below Planck mass (every known particle, every atom): Compton dominates
  - Above Planck mass (stars, black holes): Schwarzschild dominates
- Punchline: we live in the Compton world; gravity is negligible at particle scales because the Compton size dwarfs the gravitational size by tens of orders of magnitude


## 2. The inversion

- Table: proton (938 MeV, r_s = 10⁻⁵⁴ m, λ_C = 1.3 fm), electron (0.511 MeV, 10⁻⁵⁷ m, 2,400 fm), neutrino (~0.03 eV, 10⁻⁶⁷ m, ~42 μm)
- Mass ranking: proton "big," electron "small," neutrino "tiny"
- Compton ranking: neutrino enormous (42 μm — visible under a microscope), electron medium, proton tiny
- The hierarchy flips completely — heaviest = smallest, lightest = largest
- Which ranking is more physical?
  - Compton wavelength determines what we actually measure: interference patterns, tunneling distances, scattering cross-sections, atom sizes
  - Mass is a number we extract from those measurements
  - The Compton scale is the measurement; mass is the inference


## 3. The Compton size is primary

- Setup: photon in free space — travels at c, has energy and momentum, no mass, no rest frame
- Confine the photon to a closed path (loop, cavity, torus)
  - Photon still travels at c on the surface
  - But the cavity's center can sit still — there IS a rest frame now
  - Rest-frame energy E = hf → mass m = hf/c²
  - The photon hasn't changed; confinement made it look like stationary matter
- The Compton wavelength is the confinement scale — the circumference of the cavity
  - Heavy = tight loop = high frequency = small λ_C
  - Light = wide loop = low frequency = large λ_C
- Key reframe: mass isn't a substance, it's a frequency; the "amount of stuff" is zero — just a photon going in circles
- Everything inside the Compton scale is "stopped" from outside; the internal motion is hidden below the resolution limit


## 4. The uncertainty zone IS the particle

- Below the Compton wavelength, you cannot localize the particle (Heisenberg)
- Two interpretations, same predictions:
  - **Point view:** particle is a point (or very small); uncertainty is our ignorance of where it is inside the Compton zone
  - **Compton view:** particle IS the Compton-scale structure; there is nothing smaller to find; the "probability cloud" isn't hiding a ball — it IS the ball
- Both are experimentally indistinguishable — they agree on all measurable predictions
- But they launch different research programs:
  - Point view → what's the point made of? what gives it mass? → Higgs mechanism, 25 free parameters
  - Compton view → what's the cavity shape? what modes does it support? → geometry, 4 free parameters


## 5. What falls out

- If the particle is a photon confined to a Compton-scale torus, properties emerge from the shape:
- **Charge:** photon phase winds 2π around the torus tube — topological twist that surrounding space sees as electric field; one winding = one unit of charge; integer because you can't have half a twist on a closed loop
- **Spin:** ratio of tube windings to ring windings; electron: 1 tube per 2 ring trips = spin ½; not an abstract quantum number — a geometric ratio on a donut
- **Magnetic moment:** circulating charge = current loop; torus carrying charge around its surface produces dipole; follows from geometry, no extra postulate
- **Inertia:** push the particle through space → photon inside must also move through space; but photon's total speed is always c; spatial motion steals from internal circulation; resistance to acceleration = mass; E = mc² is the Pythagorean theorem splitting velocity between internal (mass) and external (momentum)


## 6. Why we default to the mass view

- Not a criticism — an explanation of the history
- **Scattering experiments probe small distances:** higher energy → smaller wavelength → things look more "point-like"; the theoretical framework naturally follows: particles are points, structure is fields around the point
- **QFT works spectacularly:** point-like interactions → predictions verified to 12 decimal places (electron g−2); when something works that well, you don't question the starting assumptions
- **The Standard Model axiomatizes mass:** "these particles exist with these masses" is an axiom; the Higgs mechanism explains HOW mass arises (coupling to a field) but not WHY each particle has the mass it does; 25 measured constants are inputs, not derivations
- The key insight: neither starting point is wrong — they differ in what's bedrock
  - SM: particles and their masses are bedrock; geometry is derived
  - Compton/MaSt: geometry is bedrock; particles and masses are derived


## 7. What the mass view misses

- When you start from "these massive particles exist," you inherit the blind spots of that axiom
- **Dark matter as a case study:**
  - Decades of searching for a new massive particle — because the axiom says particles have mass, so missing mass must be a missing particle
  - WIMPs, axions, sterile neutrinos — all postulated as new entries in the particle table
  - But in the Compton/geometric view, particles are modes on a cavity; most modes on any geometry are electrically neutral — they don't couple to light, so we can't see them
  - Dark matter isn't a missing particle; it's the uncharged majority of the same spectrum that gives us visible matter
  - The same geometry that produces electrons and protons also produces neutral modes — they were always there, just invisible to electromagnetic detection
- Broader point: sometimes starting from a more fundamental level reveals what's invisible from above; the mass view doesn't predict dark matter should exist; the geometric view predicts it must


## 8. What changes

- Summary of the shifted picture:
  - Particles are structures, not points — extended energy at the Compton scale, as physical as a drumhead
  - Mass is emergent, not fundamental — the frequency of a confined photon; change the geometry, change the mass
  - The particle zoo is a mode spectrum — different standing-wave patterns on the same cavity, like different notes on one instrument
  - The mass hierarchy is a size hierarchy (inverted) — proton heavy because its cavity is tight; neutrino light because its cavity is wide
- What MaSt derives from this starting point:
  - 18 of 20 particle masses from 4 dimensionless inputs
  - Three generations from one geometric parameter
  - Nuclear masses to 1%
  - Dark matter as a natural byproduct, not a bolt-on
- Closing: the Compton view isn't just a reinterpretation — it might be the more natural starting point
